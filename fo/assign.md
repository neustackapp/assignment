# Founder’s Office Take-Home Exercise

## Overview
You are working in the Founder’s Office at a fast-growing B2B SaaS company serving enterprise customers in a regulated industry.

It is **Tuesday, 8:00 AM**. The founder is traveling and has limited availability for the next 24 hours. You are the primary person helping keep several important threads moving.

You will find a packet of materials in the `packet/` folder. Your job is to read, synthesize, prioritize, and respond as if you were already in the role.

## Deliverables
Please submit the following:

### 1. Founder briefing memo
Write a **2-page max memo** for the founder that covers:
- how you would prioritize the situations in the packet
- what you would do in the next 24 hours
- what you would escalate to the founder vs handle yourself
- key risks, tradeoffs, and assumptions
- where speed matters most vs where caution matters most

### 2. Draft communications
Draft the following:
- a short customer email acknowledging contract redlines and preserving momentum
- a concise note to outside counsel summarizing key legal issues and questions
- a founder-ready hiring summary with a recommendation
- a short internal recommendation to HR on how to proceed
- a compliance follow-up message to delayed owners

### 3. Action tracker
Create a simple working tracker in table form with:
- issue
- owner
- priority
- next step
- deadline
- founder involvement needed
- risk if delayed

### 4. Optional judgment section
In **5–8 bullets**, list:
- common mistakes companies make in situations like these
- the principles you would use to avoid those mistakes

## Time guidance
Please spend **no more than 90 minutes** on this exercise.

We are not looking for polished slides. We are looking for:
- judgment
- prioritization
- clarity
- strong written communication
- practical execution

It is okay to make reasonable assumptions. Please state them where relevant.
